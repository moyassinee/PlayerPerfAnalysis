Overview

THE PROBLEM

Mohamed Salah is one of the most complete attacking players in Premier League history. This project scrapes FBref data using soccerdata, aggregates statistics across multiple seasons, merges shooting, passing, defense, and possession tables, then computes cosine similarity to rank the statistically closest forwards across all Big 5 European leagues.

Multi-season aggregation reduces noise from one-off performances, giving a more robust picture of a player's sustained attacking profile. A secondary filter isolates players under 23 outside the Premier League — targeting affordable, high-upside replacements.

                    "Ast_", "xAG_", "Expected_xA", "KP_", "1/3_", "PPA_",
                    "CrsPA_", "PrgP_", "Tackles_Tkl", "Tackles_TklW",
                    "Take-Ons_Att", "Take-Ons_Succ",
                    "Carries_PrgDist", "Carries_PrgC", "Carries_1/3"]

# Keep most recent team & age for display
most_recent = (
    df.sort_values("season_", ascending=False)
    .groupby("player_")[["team_", "age_", "league_", "pos_"]]
    .first()
    .reset_index()
)

# Sum cumulative stats across all seasons per player
df_agg = (
    df.groupby("player_")[cumulative_cols]
    .sum(min_count=1)   # NaN if player has no data at all
    .reset_index()
    .merge(most_recent, on="player_", how="left")
)

# Filter: min 20 combined 90s across all seasons
df_agg = df_agg[df_agg["90s_"] >= 20]
Why aggregate?

Summing across seasons before normalising to per-90 gives a smoother signal — a player who scored 10 goals in 900 minutes over two seasons is ranked equally to one who scored 10 in 900 minutes in one. Single-season samples can be heavily skewed by injuries, form, or fixture difficulty.

from sklearn.metrics.pairwise import cosine_similarity

# Locate Salah's row using np.where on df_agg
salah_pos    = np.where(df_agg['player_'] == 'Mohamed Salah')[0][0]
salah_vector = X_scaled[salah_pos].reshape(1, -1)

# Compute cosine similarity against all aggregated forward vectors
similarities              = cosine_similarity(salah_vector, X_scaled)[0]
df_agg['similarity_score'] = similarities

# Rank all forwards (excluding Salah himself)
similar_players = (
    df_agg[df_agg['player_'] != 'Mohamed Salah']
    .sort_values('similarity_score', ascending=False)
)

Design Decisions

KEY CHOICES

◈
Multi-season aggregation
Stats summed across all seasons via groupby — reduces single-season noise, rewards sustained performers
🗓
Most-recent meta
Team, age, league pulled from latest season — player could have moved clubs between aggregated seasons
⚡
Per-90 normalisation
Divides cumulative stats by combined 90s — Sh/90 & SoT/90 excluded as they are already rate-based
📐
Cosine over Euclidean
Measures direction of play style, not absolute volume — a player can match Salah's profile at a weaker club
🎯
Position pre-filter
Only FW and FW,MF roles — prevents CDMs or CBs polluting the similarity space
🔢
≥20 90s threshold
Combined across seasons — stricter than single-season cut to ensure statistical reliability at aggregate level
🔗
Multi-table merge
Shooting, passing, defense, possession joined on player + season + team for a complete attacking profile
🩹
Median imputation
Missing values filled with column median before scaling — avoids dropping players with partial records
